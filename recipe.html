Recipe Recommender System Using Image Recognition of Food Ingredients

machine learning, convolutional neural network, image recognition, recommendation system.

What's for dinner? I always ask me this question when I decide to make food at home. I opened a cooking app and find something looks delicious. But when I checked the ingredient list, I found that I missed some of them. I had to give up this choice and look for something else. This process frustrate me a lot. Though there are several recipe apps and websites letting users search by keywords. They fail at identifying users' contrains, i.e., limited food ingredients choices. To help users avoid this problem, image recognition can be employed to identity food ingredients that are available at their repository and search recipes based on that.

We developed a web application that let people upload ingredients pictures and perform image recognition on them. The search system will find a recipe contains as many as the recognized ingredients. We built a convolutional neural network model to recognize five categories of food ingredients and achieved 62.9% accuate.

In potential application, we hope users can use their mobile phone to take a picture of all the ingredients they have and recommend recipe based on that. Even more, this system can be embedded in the smart refrigerator to help people decide what is for dinner.
